Why Choose FInland

Safe and High-Quality Life

Finland is one of the safest countries in the world and boasts a high standard of living. Public services, like healthcare and public transport, are excellent and often discounted for students.

High Quality Education

Finland consistently ranks among the top countries for education worldwide, with its system emphasizing innovation, critical thinking, and collaboration. Finnish universities and universities of applied sciences (UAS) are highly respected and often rank well globally. An average satisfaction rating of 9.2 out of 10 by the global platform StudyPortals.

Focus on Research and Innovation

Finland excels in areas like technology, sustainability, and design. Universities often partner with industries, providing students with hands-on experience and opportunities for innovation.

Affordable Education

For non-EU/EEA students, while there are tuition fees, they are often lower than in many other countries, and scholarships are widely available.

Work Opportunities

International students are allowed to work part-time during their studies (up to 30 hours a week) and full-time during holidays.Finland is actively looking for skilled professionals in fields like IT, engineering, and healthcare, which can be an advantage for students after graduation.

Sustainability and Well-Being

Finland is a global leader in sustainability and environmental consciousness. The emphasis on work-life balance and student well-being is reflected in the education system and broader society. Dependents can work full-time and children can get free education.

Key Advantages

Finland's universities rank highly globally, offering innovative, research-driven, and practical education. Fields like engineering, business, and technology are particularly renowned​. Students have autonomy in choosing courses and designing their study paths. Small class sizes foster personalized interactions with professors. Clean air, pristine nature, and a strong emphasis on sustainability provide a healthy and inspiring environment. Institutions organize orientation programs, cultural events, and Finnish language courses to help international students integrate​. Students can tailor their learning experiences through interdisciplinary programs and flexible curriculums​Practical learning opportunities, such as internships and industry collaborations, enhance employability​.

Education System

Finland's education system is globally recognized for its quality. Nine universities are ranked in the QS World University Rankings 2024, including Aalto University and the University of Helsinki. Programs encourage students to develop business ideas, with startup grants and networking opportunities available.

Finland's education system is internationally acclaimed for its innovative and student-centric approach. Here are the details about its structure, focus areas, and features, including joint and separate programs, research-based education, and its flexibility.

Education System in Finland

Dual Model of Education

  • Universities : Offer research-based education with a focus on academic and theoretical studies. Suitable for those aiming for master's or doctoral degrees.
  • Universities of Applied Sciences (UAS) : Provide practical and vocational training, emphasizing industry-relevant skills and professional competence. UAS includes bachelor's and master's programs requiring prior work experience for advanced degrees.

Universities emphasize critical thinking, problem-solving, and innovative research in various fields, such as technology, health sciences, and environmental sustainability. Collaborative projects between academics and industries help translate research into practical solutions.

Application Methods

Finland offers two primary application methods for degree programs: Joint Application and Separate Application.

  • Joint Application: Studies starting in autumn (September) are arranged annually in January. Students can apply to up to six-degree programs with one single application. This process involves :
    • Phase 1: International UAS Entrance Exam
    • Phase 2: Invite for further assessment upon qualification
  • Separate Application: Some degree programs have application periods open before or after the joint application periods. This process includes:
    • Group discussions or entrance tests, depending on the program and UAS.

No rigid age or gap requirements, accommodating students from diverse backgrounds.

Equal access to education, focusing on equity and minimizing competition pressures.

Finland’s education system is versatile, catering to various academic and professional needs. Its blend of research-based and application-focused education, combined with rolling admissions and flexible curriculum, makes it highly accessible and effective for students worldwide. This system equips students with skills for both academic success and global employability.
